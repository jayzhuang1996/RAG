Meet the Cast of Characters

Every file in this codebase has one job. Here's who everyone is and what they're responsible for.

Think of it Like a Restaurant Kitchen

A professional kitchen has specialized roles: the expediter takes orders, the prep cook does the chopping, the line cook handles the actual cooking, and the sommelier pairs the wine. No one person does everything — each role has a defined responsibility.

Your RAG app is the same. Here are the six main "staff members" and their roles:

📁 RAG/
📄 main.py — The expediter: takes your command and routes it to the right cook
📄 api.py — The front-of-house: accepts HTTP requests from external apps
📁 src/
📄 ingest.py — The prep cook: orchestrates the full ingestion workflow
📄 scraper.py — The forager: fetches YouTube video lists from channels
📄 transcripts.py — The transcriptionist: downloads spoken words as text
📄 chunking.py — The knife work: slices long transcripts into searchable pieces
📄 embeddings.py — The translator: converts text into numbers the database understands
📄 retrieval.py — The detective: finds the most relevant passages for any question
📄 query.py — The head chef: coordinates retrieval + prompt building + AI call

The Two Databases

Your app actually uses two different storage systems, and each is optimized for a different kind of search. Think of them as two different card catalogues in the same library:

🗄️

ChromaDB (or Supabase)

Stores text chunks as vectors — numbers that capture the meaning of each passage. Finds results that are semantically similar to your question, even if they use different words.

📝

SQLite / BM25 Index

Stores raw text and builds a keyword index. BM25 finds results that contain the exact words from your question — great for proper nouns like names and company names.

💡
Why use both?

If you search "Altman on AGI timeline," vector search finds semantically related passages even if they say "artificial general intelligence schedule." BM25 finds passages that literally contain "Altman." Using both together — called hybrid search — catches results that either method alone would miss.
